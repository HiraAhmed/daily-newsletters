**Summary:** 
The DeFi sector has seen a significant downturn, with $13.21 billion wiped from its total value locked (TVL) due to a $292 million exploit of KelpDAO's bridge. The exploit led to the use of compromised rsETH tokens as collateral across various lending platforms, causing market freezes and massive user withdrawals. The situation highlights the vulnerabilities in cross-chain bridge verification processes and the systemic risks inherent in the current infrastructure of DeFi.

### Aave Exploit Sees $10.1B Withdrawn Post-rsETH Attack
**Source:** [https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/69e5806ec7af2d69d924f6f8/](https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/69e5806ec7af2d69d924f6f8/)

**Summary:** 
Aave experienced a massive $10.1 billion withdrawal following an exploit involving the rsETH token, managed by Kelp DAO. The exploit led to the unauthorized minting and bridging of rsETH, which was then used as collateral to borrow other assets on Aave. Aave's total value locked (TVL) dropped by over 22%, from $45.8 billion to $35.7 billion, and the annual percentage yield (APY) for stablecoin suppliers surged to 13.4% due to the scarcity of lendable assets. The incident underscores the fragility of liquidity in the DeFi ecosystem and prompts a broader reassessment of security and risk across similar protocols.
    
### Moody’s Views on Stablecoins Impacting Banks Soon
**Source:** [https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/69e5644eb14b9d08a7fa4b23/](https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/69e5644eb14b9d08a7fa4b23/)

**Summary:** 
Moody's analysis suggests that stablecoins are not expected to pose a significant threat to traditional banks in the near term, primarily due to policy constraints. While the market for stablecoins and tokenized real-world assets (RWAs) is expanding, their impact on the banking sector remains modest. The potential for these digital assets to disrupt traditional banking grows as their adoption increases, particularly in on-chain payments and cross-border transactions. The fate of the CLARITY Act, crucial for providing a clear regulatory framework for stablecoins, remains uncertain and could influence the pace of innovation and the level of institutional adoption of these digital assets.
