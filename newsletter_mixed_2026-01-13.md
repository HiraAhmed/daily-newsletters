### South Korea Ends Ban on Corporate Crypto Investment
**Source:** [https://cointelegraph.com/news/south-korea-ends-9-year-ban-on-corporate-crypto-investment](https://cointelegraph.com/news/south-korea-ends-9-year-ban-on-corporate-crypto-investment)

**Summary:** 
South Korea's Financial Services Commission plans to revise regulations to allow corporate investment in cryptocurrencies, reversing a 2017 ban. Listed companies and professional investors will be allowed to allocate up to 5% of their equity capital into the top 20 cryptocurrencies by market cap. The regulatory change, expected to be implemented by early 2023, could channel significant funds into the Korean crypto market and boost local blockchain startups. The South Korean government is also advancing its digital currency strategy, aiming to execute 25% of all national treasury transactions through a central bank digital currency by 2030.

### Senate Readies Crypto Market Structure Bill
**Source:** [https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/6963f1840c9dc14f18207cb2/](https://coinmarketcap.com/community/articles/6963f1840c9dc14f18207cb2/)

**Summary:** 
Senate committees are expected to advance a legislative proposal aimed at refining the regulatory framework governing the cryptocurrency market by mid-January 2026. The new bill, House Bill 3633, proposes a structured division of oversight responsibilities between the Commodity Futures Trading Commission and the Securities and Exchange Commission. The legislation is designed to provide clear regulatory guidelines that could potentially attract more institutional investors by enhancing legal compliance for digital commodities and assets. This move is seen as a pivotal step towards stabilizing the crypto market and encouraging broader participation by ensuring a more secure investment environment.
